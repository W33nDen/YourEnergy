Daily norm of sports

The World Health Organization recommends at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic physical activity throughout the week for adults aged 18-64. However, what happens if we are on the road to reaching the goal of 150 minutes (2.5 hours)? What happens if we only have 30 minutes (or 15, or 45)? Is this still considered moderate-intensity aerobic activity? What are the benefits of a single bout of exercise? Here is where the concept of a 'daily bout' of exercise helps. Exercise specialists usually speak in terms of 'daily bouts' per person to be doing with their daily well-being and overall quality of life.
